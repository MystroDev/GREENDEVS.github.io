Consortium CIPROMED

As part of the CIPROMED consortium, we work with 17 partners representing ten Euro-Mediterranean countries including eight industrial partners and eight research partners, including universities as well as internationally recognized research institutes. CIPROMED brings together different experts from different fields, from biotechnology, agriculture, aquaculture, food engineering and food production, working together to explore and exploit the results obtained in the Mediterranean region and beyond .

Missions

The Green Green Development and Innovation association works in the following areas:

Sustainable agricultural development

We work towards the development of sustainable agriculture on a local, regional and national scale, exploring various areas such as ecology, technology and renewable energy. Our participatory and inclusive approach with local stakeholders is based on the adoption of modern, innovative and environmentally friendly technologies.

Protection of the environment and ecology

Our commitment focuses on preserving the environment, safeguarding natural resources and protecting biodiversity in the face of ecological, anthropogenic and climatic threats.

Economic development

Our association plays an active and committed role in promoting economic development, particularly in rural areas where it is of crucial importance. We work to encourage structuring projects which not only stimulate local economic activity but also contribute to environmental and social sustainability. This includes supporting farmers to adopt innovative and environmentally friendly agricultural practices, as well as promoting local and sustainable supply chains. We seek to create employment opportunities, strengthen local skills and encourage the involvement of young people and women in sustainable economic initiatives.

Research and Development

We carry out research activities and experimental trials in collaboration with research institutions and universities nationally and internationally through the involvement of trainees and doctoral students in different subjects. Our research activities focus on crucial sectors such as agronomy, agri-food, plant and animal production, ecology and the environment. Our research activities aim to explore new innovations to enhance sustainability and productivity in these areas. /p>

Communication, dissemination, awareness and training

We actively contribute to raising awareness and training individuals and organizations who share our goals. Our commitment includes strengthening the skills of farmers, associations and cooperatives at local, regional and international levels. As part of. Our communication and dissemination activities, we organize conferences, forums, seminars and publish research and periodicals in national and international newspapers and journals.

Partnership and cooperation

We promote coordination and cooperation with various national and international organizations, institutes and industrialists. The exchange of experiences and the implementation of joint activities within the framework of cooperation projects constitute our contribution to the development of large-scale positioning policies.

Presentation

Presentation

The Green Development and Innovation Association (GDI) is a non-profit, non-governmental organization working in the development of sustainable and ecological agriculture, the preservation of the environment, the protection of natural resources and biodiversity in the face of dangers likely to cause environmental or ecological imbalances, in the current context of climate change. The contributes to sustainable development at local, regional and national levels in various agricultural, environmental and renewable energy fields, by adopting renewable approaches, modern, innovative and environmentally friendly technologies. Its objective is to contribute to the promotion of economic, social and environmental development, particularly in rural areas. The association also contributes to raising awareness of issues related to the environment, ecology, agriculture and sustainable development by involving young people and giving particular importance to the role of women in sustainable development.

Our projects

CIPROMED Project Challenge Solution for well-being.

The CIPROMED project (Circular and Inclusive utilization of alternative PROteins in the MEDiterranean value chains) is part of the association's international cooperation activities in the field of Research and Development which aims to support the association's position in the Mediterranean region. The project includes 17 partners (the Green Development and Innovation association is a partner) from ten Mediterranean countries including eight industrial partners and eight Research and Development partners. The CIPROMED project aims to: - Implement a circular approach model for the use of alternative protein sources for the Mediterranean agri-food system. - Increase the stability and resilience of Mediterranean agri-food production systems through the appropriate and direct exploitation of traditional crops produced locally

Visit of the CIPROMED project partners

On November 23 and 24, 2023, the association received a visit from our Greek, Italian and German partners as part of multilateral exchange activities between the partners of the CIPROMED project. The visit was an opportunity to discuss progress research carried out by our association as part of the project. This visit was an opportunity to visit the pilot unit for breeding and producing black soldier fly insects in Ain Taoujdate, installed as part of the ADVAGROMED project, coordinated in Morocco by the Sultan Moulay Slimane University.

The agricultural test field of the pilot unit of the Green Development and Innovation association

Launch of field trials to test the effectiveness of insect frass and chicken manure in the bio-fertilization of crops On December 26, 2023, the association launched experiments to evaluate the beneficial effect of black soldier fly frass and chicken manure in soil bio-fertilization and plant growth. and crop productivity. These tests are part of the two projects CIPROMED and ADVAGROMED in collaboration with the startup EntomoNutris. The tests were carried out on beans and barley while waiting to expand other experiments on the cultivation of lupine and onion.

Organization of the international congress “Agroecology, a new approach for sustainable and resilient food systems”

The Green Development and Innovation association, the 3BIO laboratory of the Khénifra Higher School of Technology, the University of Turin, the University of Thessaly organized the international congress entitled “Agroecology, a new approach for sustainable and resilient food systems” which was held on December 21 and 22, 2023 in Khenifra. The congress was an opportunity to interact with several expert speakers, researchers, academics, doctoral students, industrialists and decision-makers in a scientific and collective debate to monitor the transition and progress of agroecological practices and technologies fully aligned with contemporary practices of the circular economy. The congress was attended by the vice-president of Sultan Moulay Slimane University and Mr. the director of the Khénifra Higher School of Technology.

Participation of the association in the Arab Summit of SMEs

The president and advisor of the Green Development and Innovation association participated in the Arab SME Summit which was held in Marrakech during the days of December 12 to 14, 2023. The Summit constituted a regional platform aimed at empowering small and medium-sized businesses of the Arab region, to improve their performance and expand their potential beyond borders. The participation of our association comes within the framework of the actions communication and dissemination of our activities carried out within the framework of the CIPROMED project. The members of the association had the opportunity to meet and discuss with the director of the PRIMA foundation, the financing program and backer of the CIPROMED project.
